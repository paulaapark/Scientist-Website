About

Nigel Braun was born in Montreal on September 7, 1991 and graduated from McGill in 2014 with a B. Sc. in Biochemistry.

In 2014 he also started his first YouTube channel 'NileRed'.
His channel features educational videos showcasing fascinating applied chemistry reactions, balancing both theory and purpose.

In 2015 he started his M. Sc. at McGill but decided to pursue his YouTube career full-time instead. Nigel's goal with his channel is to showcase how fun and interesting chemistry can be and contradict how it is taught in most academic settings.

As of 2022, Nigel has over 4.1M subscribers on NileRed alone and has since created a couple more channels that share chemistry content in slightly different formats.

Publications


Turning Plastic Gloves into Hot Sauce

Turning plastic gloves into hot sauce is something that I've been wanting to do since I started this channel. I never attempted it though because I knew that it was going to be the longest and hardest project I've ever worked on. However, one day I randomly decided that I was ready and I just got started on it...


Turning cotton balls into cotton candy

For a couple years now, I've been wanting to turn cotton balls into edible (and literal) cotton candy. It took me a while to figure out how to do it, but I finally did it! This is kind of a follow-up to my previous project, where I turned toilet paper into drinkable alcohol (moonshine). In that one, I used an enzyme called cellulase, but that didn't seem to work here. So, instead, I used concentrated sulfuric acid.


Making transparent wood

A few years ago I tried making transparent wood and it kind of worked. The pieces that I made were somewhat okay, but I was never really happy with the result. I finally decided to try it again though!
